# Getting Started

This guide walks you through creating your account, verifying your email, signing in for the first time, and understanding your dashboard. If you have been added to the platform by your organization's administrator, skip to [First Login](#4-first-login).

---

## 1. Account Types

GradeOps AI supports two ways to get a teacher account:

| Account Type | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Self-registered** | You sign up directly at `/register`. Your account is ready after you verify your email. |
| **Operator-provisioned** | Your organization's admin creates your account on your behalf. You receive login credentials or a setup email. No registration step required. |

Both account types give you the same teacher workspace. The difference is only in how the account is created.

---

## 2. Self-Registration

If your organization has not set up your account, you can register yourself in under two minutes.

1. Go to `/register` in your browser.
2. Fill in your full name, email address, and a password.
3. Click **Create account**.
4. The system creates your account using Firebase Authentication (your password is never stored on GradeOps AI servers) and registers your teacher profile.
5. You are redirected to the email verification screen.

**Notes on your password:**
- Choose a strong, unique password. GradeOps AI enforces minimum password requirements set by Firebase.
- If you try to register with an email that already has an account, you will see a clear error message.
- GradeOps AI never stores, logs, or transmits your raw password.

---

## 3. Email Verification

Email verification confirms that your address is real and reachable. This is required before you can access the teacher workspace.

### What you see after registering

After registration, you are redirected to a pending-verification screen. This screen shows:

- A message confirming that a verification email has been sent to your address.
- A **Resend verification email** button, in case the email does not arrive.
- An **I've verified my email** button, which you click after confirming your email to proceed to your workspace.

### Steps to verify

1. Open your email inbox and look for a message from GradeOps AI with the subject line containing "verify" or "confirm."
2. Click the verification link in the email. This link opens a browser tab and marks your address as verified.
3. Return to the GradeOps AI tab and click **I've verified my email**.
4. You are redirected to your dashboard.

### If the email did not arrive

- Check your spam or junk folder.
- Wait up to two minutes, then click **Resend verification email**. The resend is rate-limited to prevent misuse.
- Make sure you registered with the correct email address. If you made a typo, register again with the correct address.

### What happens if you try to access the dashboard without verifying

The platform blocks unverified access at both the frontend and API level. If you try to navigate to the dashboard before verifying, you are redirected back to the verification screen. This check is enforced server-side — it is not possible to bypass by manipulating the browser.

---

## 4. First Login

If your account was provisioned by your organization's admin, start here.

1. Go to `/login`.
2. Enter your email address and password.
3. Click **Sign in**.
4. If your email is verified, you are taken directly to your dashboard.
5. If your email is not yet verified, you are redirected to the email verification screen.

**Wrong credentials:** If you enter an incorrect email or password, you will see an error message. The system does not specify which field is wrong, to protect account security.

---

## 5. Your Dashboard

The dashboard is your starting point every time you sign in. It gives you an operational picture of all your assessments at a glance.

### First login — empty state

When you have no assessments yet, the dashboard shows an empty state with a prompt:

> **No assessments yet.**
> Create your first assessment to start your first AI-assisted grading cycle.

A **Create assessment** link takes you directly to the assessment creation form.

### When you have assessments

Each assessment appears as a card showing:

| Card Element | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| **Title** | The assessment name, generated by the AI or set by you |
| **Status badge** | The current stage of the assessment (see table below) |
| **Submission count** | How many student submissions have been added |
| **Pending approvals** | How many AI outputs are waiting for your review |
| **Report link** | A direct link to the teacher report, shown only when the report has been generated |

### Status badge meanings

| Badge | Color | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **DRAFT** | Gray | The assessment brief has been created but the assessment is not fully configured or approved |
| **OPEN** | Blue | The assessment is active and accepting submissions or student responses |
| **GRADING** | Yellow | AI grading agents are running, or grading suggestions are waiting for your review |
| **CLOSED** | Green | Grading is complete and results have been published to students |

The **Pending approvals** count is the most important number on each card. If it is greater than zero, there is something waiting for your action — a rubric to approve, grading suggestions to review, or feedback to sign off on. When it reaches zero, you are up to date for that assessment.

## 6. Session Management

### How long your session lasts

Session lifetime is configured at the deployment level by your organization's admin. Firebase Authentication handles session tokens automatically, refreshing them in the background as you work. In practice, an active working session will not expire unexpectedly.

### What happens when your session expires

If your session expires while you are away (for example, you left the browser open overnight), the next action you take will redirect you to `/login` with a message:

> **Your session has expired. Please sign in again.**

Any work you had not yet saved (for example, an approval you were in the middle of reviewing) is not lost — your approved and saved changes are stored server-side. Only unsaved in-progress edits in a form may need to be re-entered.

### Signing out

Click the **Sign Out** button available in the workspace navigation. This immediately ends your session. After signing out, protected pages require you to sign in again before they can be accessed.

For security on shared machines, signing out also revokes your refresh token server-side, so no further automatic session refreshes can occur.

---

## 7. Account Security

- **Never share your password.** Your account is scoped to your assessments only. Another teacher cannot see your assessments, and you cannot see theirs.
- **Sessions are managed securely by Firebase Authentication.** GradeOps AI never stores credentials on its own servers.
- **Sign out explicitly** when using a shared or public machine. The sign-out action revokes your session immediately.
- **Password reset** is available at `/forgot-password`. Enter your email and follow the link sent to your inbox.
