# GradeOps AI — Teacher User Guide

## What is GradeOps AI?

GradeOps AI is an assessment operations platform for programming educators. It lets you run complete assessment cycles — from defining a learning goal through grading, feedback, learning gap detection, and reporting — using a pipeline of AI agents. The result is faster turnaround, more consistent grading, and higher-quality feedback for your students, without requiring you to give up control over any pedagogical decision.

GradeOps AI is designed for programming instructors, independent tutors, and bootcamp teachers who process practical assignments or objective knowledge checks on a regular basis. If you spend hours grading code submissions or writing individual feedback, this platform is built for the pain you feel.

The platform is teacher-led from start to finish. AI agents draft assessments, suggest grades, write feedback, summarize learning gaps, and propose recovery activities. You review every output, edit what needs changing, and approve what goes forward. Nothing reaches your students without your explicit sign-off.

## Two Assessment Modes

**Open Assessment** is designed for practical, code-based, or open-ended tasks. You describe the learning goal, the AI generates an assessment draft and a grading rubric, and you upload student submissions. AI agents analyze each submission against your approved rubric and produce grading suggestions and personalized feedback. You review and approve every suggestion. This mode is best for programming exercises, code reviews, and extended written tasks.

**Closed Assessment** is designed for objective knowledge checks: True/False, single-choice, and multiple-choice questions. AI agents generate a question bank, you curate it, and the system assembles a balanced assessment. Students receive a unique secure link to respond — no account required. Grading is fully deterministic, calculated automatically against a frozen answer key. You review exceptions and publish results. This mode is best for conceptual checks, theory assessments, and quick knowledge verifications.

## The Core Principle: AI Assists, You Decide

GradeOps AI generates suggestions and drafts. You make final decisions. This is not a design constraint — it is a deliberate pedagogical principle.

- No grade is final until you approve it.
- No feedback reaches a student without your explicit approval.
- No rubric is used for grading unless you have locked it.
- Every AI output is labeled as a suggestion until you act on it.

You can always edit, reject, or request a regeneration of any AI output. Your edits and approvals are tracked in an audit log, giving you a clear record of every decision made during an assessment cycle.
